Weather Conditions and Crops

1. Spring

Weather Conditions: Spring is a transitional season between winter and summer, marked by mild temperatures, increased daylight, and moderate rainfall. It brings the melting of snow, blooming flowers, and active wildlife. The weather can be unpredictable, with occasional cold spells and storms.

Crops: Spring is ideal for planting crops like wheat, barley, oats, and potatoes. Fruits such as strawberries and cherries also begin to bloom. Farmers prepare soil and sow seeds for summer crops like corn and tomatoes.

2. Summer

Weather Conditions: Summer is characterized by high temperatures, longer daylight hours, and occasional thunderstorms. In some regions, it brings droughts, while others experience monsoon rains.

Crops: Heat-loving crops like maize, rice, sugarcane, and cotton thrive. Fruits such as mangoes, watermelons, and peaches are abundant. Vegetables like cucumbers and bell peppers grow well.

3.Autumn

Weather Conditions: Autumn sees cooling temperatures, falling leaves, and reduced rainfall. It marks the transition to winter, with shorter days and occasional frosts.

Crops: Harvest season for crops like apples, pumpkins, carrots, and soybeans. Winter crops such as wheat and rye are planted.

4. Winter

Weather Conditions: Winter is marked by cold temperatures, snowfall, frost, and shorter days. Some regions experience extreme cold and blizzards.

Crops: Cold-resistant crops like wheat, barley, spinach, and cabbage are grown. Root vegetables such as carrots and radishes thrive in mild winter conditions.

5. Monsoon

Weather Conditions: Monsoon season is defined by heavy rainfall, high humidity, and occasional storms. Flooding and landslides are common in some areas.

Crops: Water-intensive crops like rice, jute, tea, and maize thrive. Vegetables such as okra and eggplant also benefit from the abundant moisture.
